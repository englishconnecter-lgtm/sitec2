4. Five-paragraph gap-fill

Choose the correct word or phrase from each dropdown. The five paragraphs are adapted from the video topic for classroom use.

Creativity is often presented as a mysterious gift, but it can also be understood as a process. People are more likely to generate original ideas when they can explore different possibilities and work within useful .

Instead of waiting passively for inspiration, creative people often build routines that help them begin. A clear warm-up, a specific brief and a limited amount of time can make the process feel more and less intimidating.

Another important part of creativity is generating many options before choosing one. Early ideas do not need to be perfect; they can be treated as material to test, combine and later.

Breaks can also help because they allow a period of . When people step back from a problem, they may return with a clearer perspective or notice connections they had missed.

Finally, feedback is useful when it is seen as part of an process. Rather than proving whether a person is creative or not, feedback helps shape the next version of an idea.

Five discussion questions

  1. Is creativity mainly a natural talent, a learnable discipline, or the result of the right environment?
  2. Can strict limits make people more creative, or do they simply make the process more efficient?
  3. Should companies deliberately give employees unstructured thinking time, even if the results are hard to measure?
  4. Which is more damaging to creativity: fear of failure, perfectionism, pressure or distraction?
  5. How can a team encourage original thinking without producing vague or unrealistic ideas?
