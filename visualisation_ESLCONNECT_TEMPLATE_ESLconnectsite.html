Visualisation, or mental imagery, means creating a vivid experience in the mind before it happens in real life. It can involve the same used to perceive the world around us, which is why imagined experiences can feel powerful.

In sport, performers often use imagery to rehearse a movement, a race or a difficult moment. This mental practice can help them feel more prepared because the brain activates related while they imagine the action.

The video also suggests that visualisation is useful beyond sport. People can set short-term and then imagine the practical steps needed to achieve them.
